Shirley Jackson

Shirley Jackson is a theoretical physicist best known for her advancements in telecommunications. She had an influence on things such as: the portable fax, touch tone telephone, solar cells, fiber optic cables, and the technology behind caller ID and call waiting while conducting research at Bell Laboratories

Shirley Jackson is someone you look at in history that you know took one of those major steps forward. She studied for her bachelor and doctoral degree in physics at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology and went on to become the first African American women to earn a Ph.D. from MIT in any subject, as well as one of the first two black women to receive a doctorate in physics in the US.

She is looked at as one of the most influential role models in the history of science because of her willingness to overcome gender and racial bias that still separated her field at the start of her career. She has won many awards, including The Medal of Science from former President Barack Obama, but what has made her legendary is not the money or recognition she has received, but the persistance she displayed. She basically had the world against her because she was a woman of color, but she goes to display that suppressing people because of their gender or race is what will hold back advancements of society, as shown in her own advancements of the field of telecommunications. Dr. Jackson is often called the ultimate role model of science.

Accomplishments

1st African American woman to earn a Ph.D. from MIT in any subject, one of the first two black women to receive a doctorate in Physics in the US, advancements of Telecommunications, current President of the Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute, first woman and first African American woman to serve as the chairman of the US Nuclear Regulatory Commission, first black woman elected to the National Academy of Engineering, first black woman to lead a national research university.
